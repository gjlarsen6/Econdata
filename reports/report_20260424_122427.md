## Overall Macro Narrative

The models collectively describe a **late-cycle deceleration with rising tail risk**. Key signals across all model groups:

- **Recession risk is serious warning.** The recession probability model forecasts 6.5% at 6 months and **31.2% at 12 months** — a sharp nonlinear acceleration from the 0.48% baseline. Even discounting model uncertainty, the trajectory warrants attention.

- **Growth is decelerating.** Industrial production is forecast to fall from 101.79 to 96.62 over 12 months. Nonfarm payrolls, PCE, and retail sales are all contracting in the model forecasts, pointing to a broad demand slowdown.

- **Manufacturing IP is forecast to contract.** IP: Manufacturing moves from 98.00 to 96.66 — a 1.34-point decline. Sub-sector divergences (Mining vs Consumer Goods vs Business Equipment) reveal which segments are most exposed to the demand slowdown.

- **Capacity utilization is restraining capex.** Manufacturing utilization (MCUMFN) is at 75.2% — below the 80% threshold that historically triggers capital investment decisions. Firms are unlikely to add capacity in this environment.

- **Manufacturing order book is weakening.** Durable goods orders (DGORDER) are forecast to move from $315,879M to $284,744M (−9.9%) — this leads actual manufacturing output by 2–3 months.

- **Credit conditions are tightening.** C&I loans are forecast to decline from $2,828B to $2,774B (Billions of Dollars). Loan contraction typically precedes business investment declines by 1–2 quarters.

- **Sector-level employment signals confirm the industrial slowdown.** Manufacturing employment is forecast to rise from 12,842K to 14,450K. Professional services and government remain more resilient, providing a partial offset to goods-sector weakness.

- **The Fed is easing modestly.** The funds rate is forecast at 3.59% in 12 months. The yield curve is re-steepening (10Y−3M spread widening to 1.58%pts), with short-term yields falling 100+ bps — the market is pricing in more cuts than the Fed is delivering.

- **Credit is flashing caution while equities calm.** VIX is forecast to decline from 20.4 to 15.6 (equity volatility peaking), but HY credit spreads are forecast to widen from 2.95% to 3.45% — credit markets are pricing in rising default risk ahead of equities.

- **Consumer sentiment is recovering.** UMCSENT rises from 56.6 to 68.4 by year-end — the one consistent bright spot. However, actual spending (PCE, retail sales) is still contracting, suggesting sentiment may be leading a real recovery or simply reflecting equity market stabilization.

- **Commodity prices are normalizing.** WTI crude is forecast to fall ~24% from $99.85/bbl to $75.59/bbl, consistent with the demand slowdown implied by other models. PPI commodity prices also bear watching as leading input cost signals.

### Bottom Line

The models favor a soft landing as the near-term base case — consumer sentiment is recovering, financial stress is easing, and VIX is declining. However, the 12-month recession probability rising to **31.2%**, combined with contracting production, payrolls, and consumer spending, signals that the window for avoiding a harder landing is narrowing. Credit spread widening and declining C&I loan growth are the most actionable near-term risk indicators to watch.

*Model quality note: Most series have negative R² values, reflecting the difficulty of forecasting macro variables in post-2020 regime-changing data. All forecasts should be treated as directional signals with wide uncertainty bands, not precise point estimates.*
